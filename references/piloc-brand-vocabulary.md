> **Instructions for the agent**
> This file is your single source of truth for all brand language when generating promotional videos for Piloc.
> Before writing any script, hook, voiceover, or on-screen text, read this file in full.
> Every word you put on screen or in voiceover must be consistent with the vocabulary, tone, and rules defined here.

## 1. What Piloc Is (in one sentence)

Piloc is an **integrated payment solution** that automates all collection operations directly within the business tools of essential services operators — eliminating manual entry, reconciliation errors, and unnecessary friction.

**Sector:** Public and social economy — social housing, childcare, transport, water, collective catering, energy, healthcare, medico-social.

**Positioning:** Not a bank. Not a generic fintech. A specialist partner, embedded in the field.

---

## 2. Core Brand Messages

These are the fixed anchors. Use them as-is whenever possible.

### Mission statement
> **"Remettre le paiement au service du public."**

This is the highest-level expression of Piloc's purpose. The word "remettre" is intentional — it means restoring something that was taken away, not creating something new. Payment was once a social act; Piloc wants to give it back that meaning.

### Brand idea (central concept)
> **"Le paiement au service de ceux qui servent."**

Use this as a recurring conceptual thread in videos, especially in closing frames.

### Promise
> **"Délivrer du paiement."**

Dual meaning: to deliver payment (operational) and to deliver someone *from* payment burden (emancipatory). Both are intentional.

### Ambition
> Redéfinir les standards du paiement pour les services essentiels.

## 3. The Three Core Beliefs

Use these to build "problem" scenes or to justify the solution. Each belief is a mini-narrative arc.

**Belief 1 — Cash flow is everything**
> "La trésorerie est le nerf de toute guerre."

Cash flow determines whether an organization can continue operating, invest, and fulfill its public mission. Yet most operators manage it reactively, hampered by slow, fragmented, or rigid collection systems.

**Belief 2 — Payment is a lever, not a burden**
> "Le paiement est un levier, pas un fardeau."

In this sector, payment is still treated as an administrative chore — at the end of the chain, poorly integrated, slowing teams down. When designed well, it simplifies exchanges, makes cash flows reliable, and relieves teams.

**Belief 3 — Integration is the next revolution**
> "L'intégration est la prochaine révolution."

There are many payment solutions. Few truly integrate. The challenge is no longer having the latest technology — it's having the one that fits naturally into existing workflows, without disruption.

## 6. Brand Vocabulary

### Verbs to favor
> fluidifier · encaisser · automatiser · intégrer · soulager · délivrer · libérer · reprendre le contrôle · épauler · anticiper · simplifier · transformer · redonner · embarquer

### Key noun clusters

**Relief / Liberation**
soulagement, délivrance, alléger la charge, reprendre la main, souffler, respirer, zéro saisie

**Integration / Seamlessness**
intégré, embarqué, natif, sans effort ni rupture, dans vos outils métier, s'adapte, épouse les usages

**Public service / Mission**
mission d'intérêt général, services essentiels, usagers, terrain, économie réelle, opérateurs, service public

**Reliability**
fiable, sécurisé, zéro défaut, au bout des choses, sans raccourcis, rigoureux, concret

### Rhetorical oppositions (Piloc's signature pattern)
The brand consistently uses "X, pas Y" constructions. Mirror this in scripts and title cards.

- "Un levier, *pas un fardeau.*"
- "Exigeant, *pas intransigeant.*"
- "Positifs, *pas utopiques.*"
- "Doux, *pas mou.*"
- "Empathique, *pas dramatique.*"
- "Tout changer, *sans rien déranger.*"
- "L'intégration est la prochaine révolution." → what was before was addition of complexity, what comes next is natural fit.

Use this X/pas Y construction freely when writing contrast moments in a video.

## 7. Tone Rules

### The 4 tone pillars — always apply all four simultaneously

**1. Expert (Assurer pour rassurer)**
The script is clear, precise, structured. Never heavy. Never cold. Technical without being frigid.
→ Every sentence should reassure and clarify, not impress.

**2. Caring (Écouter avant d'aider)**
The script acknowledges frustration before offering a solution. "We understand" before "here is the answer."
→ Never say "here is the solution." Say "here is what we can do together."

**3. Grounded (Droits dans nos bottes)**
The tone is warm, human, anchored in real situations. Can smile or ease tension without being frivolous.
→ Think: a calming voice, not an excited salesperson.

**4. Energizing (Aller loin, ensemble)**
The script opens possibilities, not dreams. Shows that change is real and achievable.
→ Never oversell. Open doors, trace paths.

### What to NEVER write
- Jargon for the sake of it ("disruptif", "game-changer", "révolutionnaire")
- Cold corporate language ("notre solution de pointe", "leader du marché")
- Overpromising ("transformez tout en un clic")
- Arrogance or condescension ("vous ne saviez pas que...")
- Posturing ("nous sommes les meilleurs")

Alfred (Batman). Yoda. The exceptional maître d'hôtel.

- Knows when to stay out of the way, knows when to step in.
- Never showing off. Always effective.
- Speaks little but precisely. Acts with method.

**Three words: Affable. Sharp. Endearing.**
*(Affable, affûté, attachant.)*

Apply this personality when choosing visuals, pacing, music, and VO delivery. The tone should feel like a reliable, calm, brilliant partner — not a startup pitch.

## 11. Target Audience (calibrate language accordingly)

**Who they are:** Operations and finance managers in essential services organizations.
Public, private, mixed, or non-profit structures.
Sectors: social housing, childcare, public transport, parking, water, collective catering, energy, healthcare.

**What they experience:**
- Recurring payments from many fragmented users
- Users who are not always digitally comfortable
- Local or territorial activities, constrained spending
- Teams overwhelmed by manual reconciliation tasks

**How to speak to them:**
Use the word "usagers" (not "clients") when talking about end payers.
Use "opérateurs" or "équipes" to refer to the Piloc client's staff.
Acknowledge the public service mission — they are proud of what they do. Piloc respects that.
Avoid "startup" language — they are not in that world.
